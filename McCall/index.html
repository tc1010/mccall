About McCall Idaho

McCall is a resort town on the western edge of Valley County, Idaho, United States. Named after its founder, Tom McCall, it is situated on the southern shore of Payette Lake, near the center of the Payette National Forest. McCall is an all-season tourist destination for outdoor recreation. The resort town is known for its Winter Carnival, extended winters, and one of the highest average snowfalls in the state.

During the Summer months, McCall comes alive with boating, water skiing, hiking, and many other outdoor activities. Average tempeatures are mid to high 80's during the summer months and low 30's during the winter months. Fair warning, once you visit McCall, you won't want to leave.
